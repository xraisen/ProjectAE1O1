Global Population Knowledge Base

Live Updates & Key Facts

Population Milestones

  • Current world population is 8.2 billion (2025)
  • World population is projected to reach 9 billion in 2037
  • The 10 billion milestone is expected around 2060
  • Historical milestones: 7 billion (2010), 6 billion (1998), 5 billion (1987), 4 billion (1974), 3 billion (1960)

Population Growth Statistics

  • Current annual growth rate is 0.85% (2025), down from 0.97% in 2020
  • About 70 million people are added to the world population annually
  • The growth rate peaked at around 2% in the mid-1960s and has been declining since
  • Population density worldwide is approximately 55 people per km²

Regional Distribution

  • Asia: 4.8 billion people (58.7% of world population)
  • Africa: 1.5 billion people (18.8% of world population)
  • Europe: 744 million people (9.0% of world population)
  • Latin America & Caribbean: 667 million people (8.1% of world population)
  • Northern America: 387 million people (4.7% of world population)
  • Oceania: 46 million people (0.6% of world population)

Wealth Distribution Facts

  • There are approximately 58 million millionaires worldwide (1.5% of global adult population)
  • The United States is home to 22 million millionaires (38% of the global total)
  • There are about 2,781 billionaires worldwide with a combined wealth of $14.2 trillion
  • The top 10 richest people hold more wealth than the bottom 40% of the global population

Population Challenges

  • Median age worldwide is 30.9 years (2025) and rising
  • Global fertility rate is 2.24 children per woman (2025), down from 2.32 in 2020
  • Urban population comprises about 56% of the total world population
  • By 2050, it's projected that 68% of the world population will live in urban areas

Data sourced from Worldometers, United Nations Population Division, Forbes, and UBS Global Wealth Report
